A wide range of topics are covered, from computer graphics algorithms and polygon reduction to the history of astronomy and the impact of names in sports. Various technical issues are discussed, such as the use of artificial neural networks in sequence analysis, the need for a polygon reduction algorithm for marching cube surfaces, and the search for a rat cell line of adrenal gland/cortical cell-type. The history of medicine is also touched on, with references to Dr. William Horatio Bates and his controversial theories on eyesight, as well as the work of Dr. Neil Gehrels in solar system astronomy. A request for information on a stolen motorcycle and a question about the best way to copy the output of a monitor onto video tape are also included. Overall, a diverse and informative look at various topics in science, technology, and history is provided.
