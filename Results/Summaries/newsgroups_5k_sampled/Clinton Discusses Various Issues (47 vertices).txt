President Clinton's remarks to the University of Maine "Black Bears" NCAA Division I hockey champions, the White House's response to the Waco tragedy, and the potential impact of the stimulus package on job creation in Pennsylvania are discussed. The reorganization of the Office of Exploration and the Office of Space Sciences, the return of hockey to UMass, and the role of the President's Task Force on National Health Care Reform in considering a Value-Added Tax (VAT) to finance health care are also covered. The President's upcoming meeting with Japanese Prime Minister Miyazawa, his position on moving the US Embassy to Jerusalem, and his support for a wide range of issues, including civil rights and AIDS, are addressed. A letter from Kenneth D. Whitehead to President Clinton expresses outrage at the conduct of government agencies in Waco, and the Clinton Clipper algorithm and its potential impact on cryptography are discussed.
