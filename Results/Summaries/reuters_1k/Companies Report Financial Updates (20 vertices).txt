Petroleos de Venezuela, S.A. (PDVSA) is set to purchase a 50% interest in a Corpus Christi, Texas refinery and related operations from Union Pacific Corp. for approximately $33 million in cash and $60 million in crude and refined oil shipments. The deal will establish a new joint venture called Champlin Refining, where PDVSA will supply up to 140,000 barrels per day to the refinery, with the option to place an additional 50,000 barrels per day through Champlin's distribution system in 10 U.S. states. HealthCo International Inc. reported improved operating results for the fourth quarter and full year 1986, with operating earnings per share increasing to 51 cents from 43 cents. Operating net income was $3,182,000, up from $2,462,000, and revenues increased to $101.7 million from $87.0 million. Manny, Moe and Jack Inc. announced a three-for-one stock split and a dividend increase, subject to shareholder approval. CrossLand Savings FSB acquired Western Savings and Loan Co. with the assistance of the Federal Savings and Loan Insurance Corp., creating a $1.7 billion asset subsidiary.
