Granny Smith, a cultivar originating from a chance seedling, is a well-known apple variety, possibly related to the Jonathan apple. Its history is traced back to a farm in Western Australia, owned by Maud and Arthur Williams, where the original tree was discovered. This apple cultivar, with its unique characteristics, is just one example of the diverse world of horticulture and plant propagation. Trademarks, as a form of intellectual property, play a crucial role in distinguishing products and services. They can take various forms, including words, symbols, and even non-traditional marks like scents or 3D shapes.
