Heidelberg stands as a city brimming with historical and cultural significance in the heart of Germany. Its landmarks, such as the Heidelberg Castle and the Karl Theodor Bridge, are iconic. The city is home to the largest church in the region, the Church of the Holy Spirit, which attracts millions of visitors annually. Situated in the state of Baden-Württemberg, Heidelberg is part of a larger metropolitan area, the Rhine-Neckar, and is one of many cities and towns in Germany, each with its unique identity. The state of Thuringia, with its capital Erfurt, is a notable neighbor, sharing borders with several other German states. Germany's federal structure comprises sixteen states, including city-states like Berlin and area states like Bavaria, Saxony, and Thuringia, which proudly call themselves "free states."
