A wide range of topics are covered, from botany and horticulture to geography, including the history of the Chelsea Physic Garden in London, England, established in 1673, which is one of the oldest botanical gardens in Britain, known for its rock garden, the oldest in Europe dedicated to alpine and Mediterranean plants. Philip Miller, an English botanist and gardener of Scottish descent, served as chief gardener at the Chelsea Physic Garden for nearly 50 years and wrote the influential "The Gardeners Dictionary." The United States of America, a federal union of 50 states and a federal capital district, Washington, D.C., located primarily in North America, borders Canada to the north and Mexico to the south, and includes the states of Alaska and Hawaii. Various scientific concepts are explored, such as the definition of a species, the role of fluid dynamics in physics, and the concept of a hybrid in biology. The history of the town of Malus, also known as Malos, in ancient Phrygia and Galatia, and its significance as a site of martyrdom during Roman and Byzantine times, is also discussed. Antoine Nicolas Duchesne, a French botanist, is known for his observations of variation within species and his demonstration of the mutability of species. The concept of visual is also introduced.
