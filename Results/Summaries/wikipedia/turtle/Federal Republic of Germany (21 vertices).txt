Germany, officially known as the Federal Republic of Germany, is a country in Central Europe with a rich history and diverse political landscape. It is a federal state, consisting of sixteen states, including city-states like Berlin, Hamburg, and Bremen, and area states such as Bavaria, Saxony, and Thuringia. The country has a population of over 82 million, making it the most populous member state of the European Union. Germany's capital, Berlin, is the EU's most populous city, while other major cities include Hamburg, Munich, and Stuttgart, the capital of Baden-Württemberg. Germany has been a part of various political entities, such as the Holy Roman Empire and the Electorate of Hanover, which was ruled in personal union with Great Britain and Ireland. The country has produced notable figures in various fields, including naturalists like Johann Friedrich Gmelin and August Johann Georg Karl Batsch, and chemist and botanist Philipp Friedrich Gmelin. Germany is known for its traditional hymns like the Badnerlied and the official national anthem, the Deutschlandlied. The country also has a diverse linguistic landscape, with names like Jens being popular in several European countries, including Germany. Additionally, Germany's cities, such as Tübingen and Göttingen, are renowned for their universities and historical significance.
