The United States Department of Defense is a federal executive department responsible for coordinating and supervising the nation's armed forces, including the Army, Navy, Marines, Air Force, and Coast Guard. It is led by the Secretary of Defense, who holds a high-ranking position in the federal cabinet. The Deputy Secretary of Defense is the second-highest-ranking official, appointed by the President with Senate confirmation. The United States Marine Corps is tasked with conducting amphibious operations. The Type 59 main battle tank, a Chinese-produced version of the Soviet T-54A tank, was produced and served in the Chinese armed forces. The Boeing 757 is an American narrow-body airliner, while the Sikorsky UH-60 Black Hawk is a military utility helicopter used by the United States Army. Capital Airlines Flight 75 crashed in 1959, resulting in the loss of all passengers and crew. The United States Department of War, the United States Department of the Navy, and the United States Census Bureau each play significant roles in their respective areas, with the Department of War and Department of the Navy having historical significance.
