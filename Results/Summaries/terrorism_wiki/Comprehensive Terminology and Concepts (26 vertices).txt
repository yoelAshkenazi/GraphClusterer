A wide range of topics are covered, from linguistics and literature to military and legal terminology, as well as various aspects of business, economics, and international relations. Key concepts such as weapons, adjectives, prisoners of war, case law, jurisdiction, offices, business administration, texts in literary theory, Alaska Natives, the International Style in architecture, narratives, risk, military operations, trade, organizations, code names, the balance of payments, jargon, commerce, operational objectives, emerging markets, and surrender are defined and explained, offering a comprehensive understanding of their respective fields. Historical and cultural aspects, such as the earliest recorded usage of certain phrases, the development of architectural styles, and the diverse Indigenous peoples of Alaska, are also touched upon.
