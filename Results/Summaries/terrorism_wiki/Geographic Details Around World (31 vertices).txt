The Great Lakes of North America are a series of interconnected freshwater lakes, with Lake Michigan being the second-largest by volume and depth. Chicago, a major city in the US state of Illinois, is home to the Loop, its central business district, and is connected to the Chicago River and the Great Lakes-Mississippi River Basin. Berlin, the largest city in Germany, is also a state within the country. Helsinki serves as the capital of Finland and its most significant center. Kuala Lumpur is the capital and largest city of Malaysia. Fairfax Stone Historical Monument State Park in West Virginia, Arlington County in Virginia, and the Straits of Mackinac in Michigan are notable locations in the US. Germany is the most populous member state of the European Union, while Sweden is the largest Nordic country. Ivory Coast, officially Côte d'Ivoire, is a West African country with a population of over 31 million. The Horn of Africa, a geopolitical region, includes Somaliland, Somalia, Djibouti, Ethiopia, and Eritrea. Other notable urban areas include Seattle in Washington, Zagreb in Croatia, and Lomé in Togo. Jutland in Northern Europe and the Pas-de-Calais department in northern France are significant geographic regions.
