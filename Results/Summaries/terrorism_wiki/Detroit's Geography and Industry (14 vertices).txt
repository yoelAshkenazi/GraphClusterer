The central business district (CBD) is the commercial and business hub of a city, often synonymous with downtown or the city center, containing offices and commercial spaces. In Detroit, the CBD is located in the downtown area. Different types of aviation facilities, including airports, heliports, and vertiports, cater to various aircraft, such as helicopters and seaplanes. The Ambassador Bridge, connecting Detroit and Windsor, is a significant international border crossing. Detroit's nicknames include "Motor City", reflecting its automotive industry. The Renaissance Center, a complex of skyscrapers in downtown Detroit, is the headquarters of General Motors, providing insights into urban geography, aviation infrastructure, and the cultural significance of Detroit.
