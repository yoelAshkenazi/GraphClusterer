A number of notable artists and architects have left their mark on history, particularly in Europe and the Middle East. Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, a French sculptor, is renowned for his design of the Statue of Liberty. Omar bin Laden, a Saudi artist and author, is also the son of Osama bin Laden. Sir Peter Lely, a Dutch-born painter, was a prominent portraitist in England, while Pierre-François-Léonard Fontaine was a French neoclassical architect. Duiliu Marcu, a Romanian architect, was prolific during the interwar period, and Charles-Édouard Jeanneret, also known as Le Corbusier, was a Swiss-French modernist architect. Hani Hanjour and Ziad Jarrah, Saudi and Lebanese terrorists, were involved in the September 11 attacks, which also saw the participation of Marwan al-Shehhi, an Emirati terrorist. Ange-Jacques Gabriel, an architect to King Louis XV of France, left his mark on French Baroque and neoclassical architecture, and Pierre Krähenbühl served as the director-general of the International Committee of the Red Cross. These individuals have contributed to various artistic and historical contexts, showcasing the diversity and talent of European and Middle Eastern figures.
