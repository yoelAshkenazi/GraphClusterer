New York City's Midtown Manhattan is a vibrant and significant area, serving as the city's primary central business district. It spans a considerable part of the island, from the East River to the Hudson River. Midtown is home to iconic landmarks and essential infrastructure, making it a crucial hub for business, tourism, and international affairs. It encompasses various neighborhoods, each with its unique character, and is defined as the area between 34th Street and 59th Street. It includes famous locations such as Times Square, the Empire State Building, and the United Nations headquarters. The district represents the heart of the city's commercial and business activities, reflecting the diverse and dynamic nature of New York City. With its concentration of prominent buildings and tourist attractions, Midtown Manhattan is a quintessential example of a central business district, offering a blend of cultural, economic, and historical significance.
