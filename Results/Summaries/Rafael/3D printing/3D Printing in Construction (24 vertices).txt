3D printing technology has found extensive applications across various industries, including the construction sector. This technology enables the creation of intricate designs and structures, revolutionizing the construction process. The advancements and challenges of 3D printing in construction, specifically with concrete materials, are notable. While the technology has made significant strides, there are still limitations, such as the inability to print ready-mixed concrete with coarse aggregates. However, researchers have developed a 3D concrete printer that addresses this issue by optimizing concrete materials. A comprehensive overview of the 3D printing process for a power distribution substation covers equipment, software, material preparation, printing, and construction inspection. This detailed study offers valuable insights and knowledge for future 3D concrete printing projects, highlighting the potential and ongoing development of this innovative construction method. The progress made in 3D printing technology for construction and the importance of overcoming existing challenges to unlock its full potential are emphasized.
