3D printing has revolutionized the creation of adsorbents through its adsorption forming strategy, offering advantages in structural design and process optimization. Various 3D printing technologies are categorized based on the type of adsorption materials used, highlighting different printing strategies such as surface impregnation, in situ growth, and mixing. The 3D printing structure can significantly impact the adsorption process, providing insights into the relationship between material properties and printing techniques. Examining these techniques and their effects enhances the understanding of 3D printing in the preparation of monolithic adsorption materials, ultimately promoting their practical applications. The potential and challenges of using 3D printing for adsorption material applications are also explored, offering a comprehensive overview of this innovative technology.
