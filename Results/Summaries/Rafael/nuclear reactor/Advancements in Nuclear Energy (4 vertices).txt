Various technological advancements and research in the field of nuclear energy and its applications are discussed. The potential of small modular nuclear reactors and desalination projects in the Middle East, North Africa, and the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia is focused on, with a techno-economic analysis of specific nuclear reactors. China's efforts in developing an accelerator-driven subcritical system for nuclear waste transmutation, including the construction of experimental loops and reactors, are highlighted. The use of CFD methods in simulating flow and heat transfer in nuclear reactors is explored, with a focus on its applications in nuclear engineering and the development of high-fidelity models. The topic of safety in fusion reactors is addressed, emphasizing the importance of establishing safety goals and reviewing the evolution of safety standards in nuclear reactors to guide the design of future fusion reactors. These efforts collectively aim to improve efficiency, safety, and environmental sustainability in the nuclear energy sector.
