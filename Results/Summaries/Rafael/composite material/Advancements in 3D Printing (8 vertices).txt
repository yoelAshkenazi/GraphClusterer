Recent advancements in 3D printing technology have focused on developing generic printing materials with enhanced properties. 3D printing has become a prevalent method for prototyping across various fields, driving the need for improved materials. The blending of materials, such as nanoparticles, fibers, and polymers, has led to the creation of high-performance composites. These composites have found applications in biomedical, mechanical, and electrical fields, offering advantages in manufacturing complex shapes at low costs. The modifications made to 3D printable materials aim to enhance their properties for both mechanical and electrical applications.
