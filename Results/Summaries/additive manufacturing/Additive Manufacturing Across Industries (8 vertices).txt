Recent advancements and applications of additive manufacturing, commonly known as 3D printing, are being utilized across various industries and educational settings. The technology brings design ideas to life, particularly in high-tech sectors like aerospace, shipbuilding, and electronics, where it can minimize production costs. Understanding and enhancing the process is a key focus, with research exploring aspects such as the use of laser polishing to improve surface quality, the introduction of submicron structures to enhance material strength and ductility, and the development of automated testing methods for quality control in fused deposition modeling. Overcoming challenges related to surface quality, external defects, and process variability is crucial for successful implementation in various fields. The technology also has potential in education, particularly for mechanical engineering students, and further research could enhance instructional methods in vocational training.
