Additive manufacturing, commonly known as 3D printing, is a versatile and innovative method for creating customized products with complex geometries and unique material properties, leading to its adoption in various industries. The technology has potential in food production, creating customized and geometrically complex food items, and is economically sustainable, providing cost-effectiveness and performance compared to conventional manufacturing. AM has been adopted across different sectors, emphasizing its economic benefits and ability to produce complex designs. Specific applications include creating energy-harvesting devices, developing biodegradable metals for bone substitution, rapid prototyping, tissue engineering, and tailored medicine. Machine learning and deep learning can optimize the AM process, and a systems design approach can evaluate its use in new design projects. Overall, AM has wide-ranging capabilities, revolutionizing various industries by offering customized, complex products.
