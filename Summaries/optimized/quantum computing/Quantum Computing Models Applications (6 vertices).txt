Various aspects of quantum computing are discussed, including its algorithms, models, and applications. The four equivalent quantum computing models are examined, with the quantum circuit model being the most prevalent, to provide a concise definition and introduce the main concepts of quantum software engineering. The design principles and methodologies of quantum algorithmic gates are explored, highlighting their simulation on classical computers and implementation on small quantum computers. A new approach for fast quantum massive parallel computing is introduced, and the development of design methods for quantum algorithm operators is discussed. The evolution of quantum computing research and its impact on various fields is covered, revealing a transition from hardware to software research and its potential for economic, industrial, and social advancements. The challenges and prospects of variational quantum algorithms and the potential of hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for NISQ devices are addressed, emphasizing the importance of error mitigation techniques.
