3D printing technology has made significant advancements and is finding applications across various industries, including construction. The potential of 3D printing in the construction industry is being explored, focusing on 3D concrete printing, which currently faces limitations such as the inability to print ready-mixed concrete with coarse aggregates. A newly developed 3D concrete printer capable of overcoming this challenge is presented. A case study of a power distribution substation explains the process, covering equipment, software, material preparation, printing, and inspection. This detailed overview contributes to the knowledge base for future 3D concrete printing projects, addressing existing challenges and offering insights into the technology's development and potential. Remaining obstacles in 3D printing construction technology are summarized, and future directions for its advancement are suggested.
