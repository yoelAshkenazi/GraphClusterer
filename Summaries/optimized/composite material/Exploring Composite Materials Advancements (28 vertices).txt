Composite materials are explored in various aspects, covering a wide range of topics, starting with an introduction that emphasizes their importance and the need for a comprehensive understanding. The relationship between materials and ergodesign is discussed, highlighting the importance of integrated design activities and the role of ergodesign in creating new objects and systems. Specific applications and advancements in composite materials are delved into, including the use of walnut shells as an aggregate in lightweight concrete, the shift from metal to carbon fiber composites in aerospace engineering, and the synthesis, analysis, and modeling of composite materials for ballistic applications. The use of composite materials in parametric architecture, the light industry, and the reinforcement of building frame elements are also examined. Additionally, the development of composite materials for various purposes, including radiation protection, and modified polymers for 3D printing are discussed, along with a new methodology for researching composite materials.
